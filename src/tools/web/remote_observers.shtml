Meet the Observers

Lynn Reitzler

Lynn is originally from Grinnell, Iowa. He became interested in astronomy by watching Star Wars and Star Trek. Lynn has co-authored a paper on asteroid rotation periods with fellow observers Brita Nellermoe and John Armstrong. In addition, he has published a paper with Brita Nellermoe concerning their research on AB Andromeda. Lynn is also the mentor for the undergraduate research group looking for new variable stars. When not studying astronomy or wrestling with the ATF's data storage problems, Lynn enjoys reading science fiction and fantasy books, and playing role playing games.

John Kazmerzak

John is a computer science major at the University of Iowa. He holds two fun and interesting jobs, one of which is working at the University of Iowa Theater Building constructing scenery, the other as an observer for The University of Iowa Automated Telescope Facility. John has recently begun a research project with David Lewis observing the comet Hale-Bopp. In the future he hopes to actively participate in the continuing expansion of the ATF and its ever increasing community of observers.

Jennifer Dahlquist

Jennifer is a senior astronomy and physics major originally from Sioux City, Iowa. Although fascinated by the stars, her other passions include music and the theater.

John Armstrong

A senior in physics and astronomy, John involves himself in a number of projects directly and indirectly associated with the ATF. In addition to being an active telescope operator and supervising a program to monitor cataclysmic variable stars, John is currently assisting with the development of a fiber fed spectrometer for the new Iowa Robotic Observatory. John enjoys outdoor activity of any nature. Originally from Denver, Colorado, John spends a large portion of his free time adjusting to life in Iowa.

Jenny Sippel

Jenny is a junior majoring in Physics and Astronomy. She has been interested in science, and astronomy in particular, for as long as she can remember. The reason Jenny is still interested in these areas is because she can't imagine anything more incredible or worthwhile than trying to figure out how the universe works. As for her hobbies, Jenny likes to read (mainly sci-fi). When she has time, she enjoys playing old school video games, especially the Sega 8-bit system. Jenny is originally from Ames, Iowa and whenever she makes it home she loves spending lots of time with her dog, Pudgy.

John Cannon

John is a junior physics and astronomy major at the University of Iowa Aside from school, John enjoys hanging out with friends, sailing and all types of sports. When home in Sioux City, Iowa, he enjoys playing with his dog and seeing his family. To put it in John's own words: "Overall, I am just an average college student trying to squeeze in as much fun as possible in my four years here; therefore, any advice taken from me should be labeled, like an unguarded swimming pool, 'Use At Your Own Risk'".

Dr. Robert Mutel

Dr. Mutel is a professor and research scientist for the University of Iowa Department of Physics and Astronomy. In addition to his teaching and research resposibilities, he is also the man behind the creation of the Automated Telescope Facility, and advisor to the undergraduate observers.

Observer Emeritus

Brita Nellermoe

Brita has left the University of Iowa to attend graduate school at the University of North Dakota. Formerly our PR person and remote observer liason, we have no illusions as to the amount of extra work her absence has placed upon us, as she has mighty big shoes to fill. She will be missed.
